Crypto Perspectives

My view on crypto—particularly Bitcoin—is evolving. In a world where fiat currencies are subject to long-term debasement and where global trust in institutions continues to erode, digital assets increasingly present a compelling alternative.

Bitcoin has become more than a speculative trade—it represents a belief system. Ethereum and Solana offer additional dimensions to the crypto ecosystem, from programmable finance to decentralized applications. These aren't just technologies—they are infrastructure for an emerging financial paradigm.

I closely follow the thinking of Raoul Pal, Julien Bittel, Andreas Steno, Jordi Visser, and Michael Saylor. While I don’t subscribe wholesale to their views, I find value in the probabilistic thinking they bring to macro, monetary systems, and digital assets.

The narrative shift is visible: increasing U.S. regulatory alignment with crypto frameworks, institutional capital flowing in, and broader public understanding of the limitations of fiat systems. As with macro trends, none of this is certain—but the signal is growing stronger.

“The future is already here — it's just not evenly distributed.”
– William Gibson

Why Crypto Appears to Be Gaining Traction

Belief & Network Effect

The more people believe in a system, the stronger and more resilient it becomes. Crypto, like all networks, thrives on shared conviction.

Dollar Debasement

Persistent fiscal expansion, rising debt, and declining purchasing power set the stage for alternatives like Bitcoin to rise.

Web3 Foundations

Ethereum and Solana are building the digital rails of the next era—smart contracts, tokenized value, and decentralized identity.
