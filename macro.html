Macro

I’m a believer in macro investing — the idea that the largest forces in the global economy tend to shape the overall direction of asset prices. Whether it’s monetary policy, demographic shifts, geopolitical transitions, or innovation cycles, the “tide” of macro movements influences nearly everything underneath it.

As the saying goes, "A rising tide lifts all boats." This quote, often attributed to John F. Kennedy, perfectly captures the dynamic where broad-based economic expansions can propel entire sectors, while contractions or dislocations can have the opposite effect. While nothing is certain in markets, some themes repeat with such frequency and structure that it becomes possible to assign a high probability to outcomes — especially when those outcomes align with structural trends.

Investors like Stan Druckenmiller and George Soros are noted for their conviction in macro themes and their willingness to bet heavily when the probabilities align. From the Plaza Accord to the dot-com boom, the global financial crisis to recent monetary distortions, macro shifts have consistently created opportunity — and risk — on a grand scale.

“It’s not whether you’re right or wrong that’s important, but how much money you make when you’re right and how much you lose when you’re wrong.” — George Soros

This page will evolve to explore current macroeconomic signals, historical parallels, and frameworks for thinking probabilistically about regime changes. Investing is never certain, but the odds are rarely equal.
